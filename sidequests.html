Important Sidequests

Many of the most interesting engineering problems in ALL-LLLM POD were not directly related to the chatbot itself. These side projects emerged while solving practical problems around transcription, speaker attribution, retrieval, dataset quality, and operational scale.

Together, they form much of the infrastructure that makes the main application possible.

Clownfish HITL console for data mapping and data extrusion Ingestion · corpus ops +

Named to celebrate getting NVIDIA NeMo working on my PC, Clownfish was created as a human-in-the-loop labeling tool that combines transcripts, audio snippets, and NeMo diarization output to map real-world identities to diarized speaker_id values.

These mappings transform anonymous speaker labels into known participants across the corpus. The same workflow also enables large-scale extraction of speaker-specific audio, creating clean datasets for downstream applications such as voice cloning, speaker classification, and persona generation.

Transcript Forensics & Repair Brody: human-in-the-loop transcript quality control. Transcript QA · lexical repair · HITL +

Brody is an extension to the Clownfish app, providing forensic tooling for identifying attribution errors, transcript corruption, segmentation mistakes, and other edge cases that can silently degrade downstream datasets.

Ghost Audio When audible speech disappears before it reaches the transcript. Transcript QA · ASR · Beam Search +

Ghost Audio investigates a failure mode where speech is present in the source audio, but vanishes from the final transcript. Using Brody and BERD-1, I traced these omissions through VAD, WhisperX, beam search, decoder probabilities, diarization boundaries, and human-in-the-loop repair workflows.

The lesson: transcript reliability is not just an ASR problem. It is an observability problem.

Memory & Retrieval Mining years of conversation for recurring arguments. Argument mining · retrieval · persona memory +

Transcripts are useful for speaker mimicry, but I also wanted to mine them for argument structures, recurring positions, and episode-level narrative arcs. The long-term goal was to generate "Black Mirror" episodes: synthetic conversations grounded in the hosts' historical reasoning patterns rather than simply their surface-level speaking style.
