Technical Product Manager - I solve problems

The original goal in 2025 was to create a helper iOS app that could rewrite my ideas in the style of other people. Text style transfer is fascinating, and a few weeks into the project I settled on the All-In Podcast Besties as chatbot personas.

Being able to mimic the Besties implied having transcripts from their podcast, which had to be created from the ground up. Transcripts required accurate speaker labeling. Accurate speaker labeling required HITL forensic review and repair. This was a balance of learning and doing. Every milestone revealed another layer, and the princess was always in another castle.

So what began as a "move fast and MVP chatbot" project turned into a serious observability project for AI pipelines and the future of training data ingestion. Transcripts have been, by far, the most challenging and rewarding part of the project. My detailed nature and the pursuit of quality and ground-truth accuracy led to a series of increasingly specialized tools.

Data Quality · Model Quality
Own the outcome.
Detailed review caught remarkably subtle data quality failure modes before they became model quality failures. Ghost Audio vanishing from transcripts without a fingerprint means the model learns the wrong relationships. Diagnosing it required reading the full pipeline — VAD, beam search, decoder behavior, embedding space — and designing a detection mechanism that could work at scale.
Job req: identify data quality failures before they become model quality failures
HITL · Tooling
Build tools you need to manage and solve the problems you have.
HITL is a product surface, not a concession. Clownfish and Brody exist because no AI transcription or classification pipeline is trustworthy — especially with super-short form audio (sub-4 seconds). Designing what the reviewer sees, hears, and how fast they move is product work. The reviewer is the user.
Job req: experience designing human-in-the-loop workflows
Tradeoffs · Hardware Constraints
Manage tradeoffs with consumer-grade hardware.
A sliding memory window was chosen over full conversational context; zero-shot F5-TTS over heavier VibeVoice LoRA adapters — not because of system limits, but because heavier configurations felt worse to use on consumer hardware. The decision criteria was feel, not spec. That's product.
Job req: make pragmatic calls under real-world constraints
Persona · Reasoning
Persona fidelity requires more than a product spec.
The argument pipeline extracts claim chains, rebuttals, and long-term positions — not quotes — because a model trained on style alone won't hold a position under pressure. Defining what "accurate" means before asking engineering to build toward it is the job.
Job req: translate ambiguous product goals into engineering-ready definitions
Corpus · Infrastructure
The corpus is the product.
560+ episodes, two pipelines, five heuristic repair patterns, Ghost Audio detection, ongoing ops. ALL-LLLM POD is cool, but the process is far more valuable. Knowing the difference — and being able to defend six months on transcription to a hiring committee — is product judgment.
Job req: wear many hats · comfortable rolling up your sleeves
