Heuristic Diarization Repair

Transcript Forensics and Repair

Transcripts were generated in an AI production pipeline using WhisperX, with NeMo adapted to operate within WhisperX's segment-level timestamps. In other words, WhisperX determined what speech existed and when it occurred, while NeMo's diarization process—identifying who spoke—was made secondary to those boundaries.

In practice, most AI errors fell into two categories: incorrect segment boundaries or speaker misclassification. Both left recognizable fingerprints in the transcript that could be surfaced through human-in-the-loop review and repaired before entering the training corpus. This review was iterated on over months, and evolved into a fast auditing workflow rather than a full episode retranscription pass.

The resulting workflow prioritized investigator time over GPU time: heuristic rules surfaced suspicious regions, generated short audio clips, and routed them for rapid review instead of requiring complete episode audits.
